_Generated by `sigil bench report` on 2026-04-16 19:52 UTC._

## Thesis

- SIGIL is not a universal compact prompt; it is a transport runtime calibrated per provider, model, and operating regime.
- The most honest benchmark today is task-level selective extended routing with plain baseline allowed as a first-class candidate.
- In that harder regime, Claude, Gemini, GPT-5.4, and now GPT-5.4-mini are all positive on aggregate total cost under the right routed policy.
- The newest OpenAI lift comes from cross-provider task-contract transfer: `gemini-nano` is now a winning family on review, architecture, and part of debugging once the local runtime absorbs its drift.
- A second new lever now compounds that: tighter per-lane output caps on already-winning families. `cap56` lanes improve OpenAI debug routing and Claude debug/refactor routing without changing the core grammar.
- A third lever now compounds both: compiled shared context. On Gemini macro, compiled cacheable prefixes plus nano task contracts and low/zero thinking budgets beat both the older long-prefix steady-state row and the plain compiled-context baseline.
- The strongest breakthrough rows still come from narrower calibrated regimes, but Anthropic is no longer limited to starter nano: the extended task-level matrix is now a clear win once transfer lanes and aggressive caps are allowed to compete across debug, review, architecture, and refactor.
- A new top-tier holdout now confirms the pattern on newer Anthropic models too: `claude-sonnet-4-6` and `claude-opus-4-6` stay positive on total cost and latency with full parseability after local rerender.
